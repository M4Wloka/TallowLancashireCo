Preparing of the Fat

Step 1

Trimming any remaining meat or blood spots from the suet. Cutting the fat into small chunks.

Boiling with Water

Step 2

Placing the fat in a large pot and add enough water to cover it. Bringing to a low boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer and stirring.

Simmering Until Fat Renders

Step 3

The fat will slowly melt, and impurities will separate. After 1-2 hours, the fat will liquefy, and the remaining tissue will float or sink.

Straining the Liquid Fat

Step 4

Pouring the mixture through a fine strainer into a heatproof bowl. Discarding the leftover cracklings.

Separate & Cool

Step 5

Letting the strained liquid sit; the tallow will rise to the top as it cools. Once solidified, lifting off the tallow block (discard the water underneath)..

Store Properly

Step 6

Transfer to glass jars and store in the fridge (lasts months) or freezer (years)
